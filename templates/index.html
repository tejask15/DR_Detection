Diabetic Retinopathy Stages

class [0]: No Diabetic Retinopathy – The eye is perfectly clear of problems caused by Diabetes.

class [1]: Mild nonproliferative diabetic retinopathy - This is the earliest stage of diabetic retinopathy, characterized by tiny areas of swelling in the blood vessels of the retina. These areas of swelling are known as micro aneurysms.

class [2]: Moderate nonproliferative diabetic retinopathy - Increased swelling of tiny blood vessels starts to interfere with blood flow to the retina, preventing proper nourishment.

class [3]: Severe nonproliferative diabetic retinopathy - A larger section of blood vessels in the retina become blocked, causing a significant decrease in blood flow to this area. At this point, the body receives signals to start growing new blood vessels in the retina.

class [4]: Proliferative diabetic retinopathy This is an advanced stage of the disease, in which new blood vessels form in the retina. Since these blood vessels are often fragile, there’s a higher risk of fluid leakage. This triggers different vision problems such as blurriness, reduced field of vision, and even blindness.
